The two-stage pattern can surface candidate risky expressions, but the model should not be trusted to explain them correctly:

- qwen-mt-flash flagged "coffee badgers" but explained it incorrectly as people frequently drinking coffee or leaving the workstation.
- qwen-mt-plus correctly recognized "coffee badgers" as workplace slang, but still gave an incomplete explanation: it framed the behavior as pretending to drink coffee to avoid work rather than the more precise hybrid-work behavior of briefly showing up or badging in to appear present.
- Both models avoided false positives on the ordinary weather sentence.
- qwen-mt-flash flagged business/domain terms such as "product-market fit" and "burn rate"; qwen-mt-plus did not, so sensitivity is inconsistent across models.
- Both models identified "kicked the can down the road" as idiomatic.

Product implication: self-review can be used as a candidate detection stage, not as final truth. Any flagged expression should be shown with uncertainty or sent through a stronger explanation/glossary lookup path before becoming user-facing canonical guidance.
